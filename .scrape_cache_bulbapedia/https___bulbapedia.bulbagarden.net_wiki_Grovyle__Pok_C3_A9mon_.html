Grovyle (Japanese: ジュプトル Juptile) is a Grass-type Pokémon introduced in Generation III.

It evolves from Treecko starting at level 16 and evolves into Sceptile starting at level 36.

Biology

Grovyle is a bipedal Pokémon similar in appearance to a theropod dinosaur. It is primarily green with a red underside. There is a belt-like green stripe across its belly. Grovyle's hands have two clawed fingers and powerful legs with birdlike feet. Grovyle has three long leaves on its wrists, a large leaf on top of its head, and two tails that have a leafy appearance.

This leafy appearance allows it to be camouflaged in the forests and dense jungles where it lives. Grovyle is said to be master at climbing trees in jungles. Grovyle's well-developed muscles make it an expert climber and allow it to leap quickly from branch to branch. The muscles are said to provide explosive bursts of power, which contribute to its incredible leaping capabilities. No Pokémon has been able to catch a fleeing Grovyle, no matter how fast they try to be. As mentioned in Sleep Style Dex, veteran researchers have difficulty finding the slumbering Grovyle due to its excellent camouflage. Grovyle has four different sleep styles: Knee-Up Sleep, Dream-Stance Sleep, Leaf-Tucked Sleep, and Atop-Belly Sleep.

In Generation III, Leaf Blade was its signature move.

NPC appearances

Spoiler warning: this article may contain major plot or ending details.
  • Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Time, Darkness and Sky: Grovyle is a major character in the main storyline. He travels to the present to prevent the collapse of Temporal Tower by stealing Time Gears, actions that make him appear villainous for most of the game. The player was his partner in the future. He also stars in the Special Episode In the Future of Darkness.
  • Pokémon Super Mystery Dungeon: The same Grovyle from Explorers of Time, Darkness, and Sky is recruitable via the Connection Orb. He requests assistance in rescuing Celebi.

In animation

Main series

Major appearances

Ash's Grovyle

Ash's Treecko evolved into a Grovyle in Exploud and Clear. In Odd Pokémon Out, Grovyle evolved into a Sceptile.

Grovyle (Explorers of Time, Darkness, and Sky)

At the start of Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Time & Darkness, a Grovyle stole one of the Time Gears. He later had a much larger role in Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Sky - Beyond Time & Darkness.

Sawyer's Grovyle

In Rivals: Today and Tomorrow!, Sawyer's Treecko evolved into Grovyle during a battle against Ash and his Frogadier. It was later revealed to have evolved into a Sceptile before A Meeting of Two Journeys!.

Other

In What You Seed is What You Get, Natasha used a Grovyle and a Slugma in a Double Battle against Ash's Corphish and Treecko. Working as a team, they were able to defeat Ash's Pokémon.

Minor appearances

In To Thine Own Pokémon Be True!, a Trainer's Grovyle competed in the Pokémon Ping Pong Tournament.

A Trainer's Grovyle appeared in HZ130, during a fantasy.

Pokémon Adventures

Main article: Emerald's Sceptile

The Treecko meant to be handed to Emerald was recovered by Wally and became a Grovyle in Dreadful Dealing with Dusclops to rescue his temporary owner when a wild Dusclops threatened to suck them into its infinite cavity. After a few more weeks of training at the Sky Pillar under Norman, he was able to awaken the ancient legendary Pokémon Rayquaza, but the huge gust of wind sent him flying miles away into a distant island where he evolved again to a Sceptile and was this time caught by Guile Hideout, who smuggled him into the Battle Factory to try to drive the nosy press away. He ended up being stolen by his legitimate owner, Emerald, and became a fixed member of his team.

Emerald had borrowed a Grovyle during his Factory challenge in Pinsir Me, I Must Be Dreaming; it should not be confused with his Sceptile. This Grovyle, like the other Factory Pokémon, is at level 50.

Trivia

  • Grovyle was the inspiration for the naming of Anyphaena grovyle, a species of spider. The species name was chosen in reference to both Grovyle and the discovered spider being found in the forest.[1]

Origin

Bulbanews has an article related to this subject:
On the Origin of Species: Treecko, Grovyle and Sceptile

Like the other members of its evolutionary line, Grovyle's design shares some similarities with the leaf-tailed gecko, a species of lizard that blends in with plantlike appendages. It also seems to be inspired by a dinosaur, bearing significant resemblance to the Dromaeosaurus. It is believed that the dinosaur had feathers, much like how Grovyle has leaves on its body - the leaves on its tail are especially similar to the tails of Epidexipteryx, which only had short tails that supported much larger feathers. Its design could be based on a Protarchaeopteryx. Its green skin and red underside seem to take inspiration from male green anoles, whose dewlaps inflate and turn red to show dominance.

Name origin

Grovyle may be a combination of grove (a group of trees), reptile, and possibly vine or ivy.

Juptile may be a combination of 樹 ju (tree) and reptile or raptor.
