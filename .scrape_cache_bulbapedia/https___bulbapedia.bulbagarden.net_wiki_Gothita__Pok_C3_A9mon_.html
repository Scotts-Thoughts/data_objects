Gothita (Japanese: ゴチム Gothimu) is a Psychic-type Pokémon introduced in Generation V.

It evolves into Gothorita starting at level 32, which evolves into Gothitelle starting at level 41.

Biology

Gothita is a small, humanoid Pokémon. A small tuft of hair sticks up from the top of its purple head, and it has two white, bow-shaped feelers on each side of the head and a third bow-shaped feeler on its neck. These ribbon-like feelers are used to amplify Gothita's psychic power, and will cause Gothita to cry if touched. It has two large blue eyes with three eyelashes on each and small red lips. Its torso is black with a white zigzagging line running horizontally through the middle. Its arms and legs are short and stubby with no visible digits.

Gothita intently observes those around it, looking at something only it can see. It can become so focused that it ends up being unaware of attacks. Gothita are normally known to be innocent. It is usually quiet, but sometimes it makes muted cries, even when alone. This is believed to be Gothita communicating with invisible beings.

In animation

Major appearances

Gothita debuted in Where Did You Go, Audino?, under the ownership of Christie. It was used to help out on the disappearance of Audino. After two Audino destroyed a bridge with Psychic so the group could not follow them, Christie commanded Gothita to use Telekinesis to get them across the river. It was then used to battle Team Rocket by using Psyshock on Woobat.

In PK22, a wild Gothita, Drilbur, and Darumaka were angry at Oshawott and Tepig, who had eaten the apples that they had collected.

In Scraggy and the Demanding Gothita!, Katharine's Gothita developed a crush on Scraggy and hoped he would join Katharine's team. However, Snivy defeated her in a battle, making her retreat.

In Eevee & Friends, a Gothita and its evolved forms were friends with an Umbreon living with an Eevee and the rest of the Eeveelutions.

In Pikachu and the Pokémon Music Squad, a Gothita was living in a forest.

In A Dancing Debut!, Nini used a Gothita in the Theme Performance of the Couriway Showcase alongside Nini's Smoochum and Farfetch'd. It helped Nini prepare a Poké Puff, advancing her to the Freestyle Performance. There, it delivered a performance along with its teammates, but it did not receive enough votes to win.

Trivia

  • Gothita can be seen as a parallel to Solosis. Both are Generation V Psychic-type Pokémon evolving once at level 32 and then again at level 41. Gothita is exclusive to Pokémon Black, Black 2, and Sword, while Solosis is exclusive to Pokémon White, White 2, and Shield. They both have the same base experience and catch rates, gain new moves at similar levels and have base stat totals of 290.

Origin

Gothita seems to be based on a child dressed in Gothic Lolita fashion, as well as the common trope to associate goth girls with magical powers or witchcraft. The Gothic Lolita fashion itself is highly influenced by the fashion from the Victorian period of the United Kingdom and also by styles from the Rococo period, the Rococo being an artistic movement born in the 18th century in France that later spread to other parts of Europe and the colonial Americas.

Due to Gothitelle's ability to predict the future from stars, Gothita and its Evolutions may be based on diviners, or more specifically astrologers. Due to their feminine appearance and association with the stars, Gothita and its Evolutions may be inspired by the Virgo sign and constellation. This is further supported by Gothorita being the Virgo equivalent in Unova's Horoscope.

Gothita was featured in an official Pokémon illustration for the Year of the Ox in 2021.[1] This is likely a pun involving its Japanese category name, ぎょうしポケモン Gyoushi Pokémon (Staring Pokémon) and うし ushi, a common reading of 牛 (cow).

Name origin

Gothita may be derived from gothic and the Spanish feminine diminutive suffix -ita. It may also be a contraction of Gothic Lolita.

Gothimu may be a combination of gothic and むちむち muchimuchi (chubby). It may also involve 予知夢 yochimu (precognitive dream).
