Scovillain (Japanese: スコヴィラン Scovillain) is a dual-type Grass/Fire Pokémon introduced in Generation IX.

It evolves from Capsakid when exposed to a Fire Stone.

Scovillain can Mega Evolve into Mega Scovillain using the Scovillainite. Mega Scovillain was introduced in the Mega Dimension DLC for Pokémon Legends: Z-A.

Biology

Scovillain is a monstrous, bipedal Pokémon. It has two bell pepper-like heads, each connected to its torso through a green, stem-like neck. While, typically, the right head is red and the left head is light green, the Pokémon Horizons: The Series episode The Knight in the Ruins revealed that, on rare occurrences, a Scovillain's heads may have the opposite color scheme or even be of the same color. The red head is depicted with an angry expression, while the light green head appears more mischievous. Both of its heads have black eyes, stick their orange tongues out, and possess short, white fangs, with the bottom pairs jutting out below their jaws. Its upper body and arms are green, and its lower body and short tail are dark green. Scovillain has a leafy growth similar to a collar at the point where the necks connect to the torso, and each of its feet end in a clawed toe.

Scovillain is described as a ferocious Pokémon whose rampage is unstoppable. The spicy chemicals in its brain stimulate both heads, turning the green head vicious while providing a source for the red head to convert into spicy, fiery energy, which it blasts its surroundings with. Scovillain's two heads have different aggressive personalities, and each head excels at different fighting styles. Despite these conflicting personalities, they act in perfect harmony and balance on the battlefield.

Scovillain is the only known Pokémon capable of learning the move Spicy Extract.

Forms

Scovillain can Mega Evolve into Mega Scovillain.

As a result of Mega Evolution, Mega Scovillain takes on a more villainous, dinosaur-like aspect. Both of its heads are more rugged and adorned with white spikes: the red head has a single row of four spikes running through the middle, while the light green head has four spikes divided between two rows above each eye, as well as two more on either side of its lower jaw. Its fangs are bigger and sharper, and its tongues have grown to the point of not fitting inside its mouths anymore. Mega Scovillain's upper body, collar, necks, and arms have turned black, resembling a business suit with a popped collar. From the middle of its collar, a jagged, glowing, necktie-like structure similar to a chili pepper hangs down. Its lower body is much bulkier, and its tail is longer as well, with three spikes near the tip. Above each of its clawed toes, there is a small spike, and the surrounding area is a darker green.

Mega Scovillain's spiciness has been further increased by Mega Evolution. In battle, it swings its pepper-like necktie to whack at its enemies.

In animation

Main series

Major appearances

A Scovillain appeared in The Knight in the Ruins, under the ownership of Pepper. It had ran away from its Trainer due to his constant use of the Strong Sphere, which caused it to enter a Laquium-induced rampage, forcing Liko to subdue it in battle before having Pagogo purify it.

Minor appearances

Scovillain debuted in Shine on, Terastallization! Liko vs. Roy!, under the ownership of a Trainer.

Discover Pokémon Together

A Scovillain appears in The Mysterious Mismagius. It attacked a Maushold before being stopped by a Mismagius.

POKÉTOON

A Scovillain appeared during a video in PT16, under the ownership of a Trainer.

In the manga

Pokémon Adventures

Scovillain debuted in Battle! Team Star, the Delinquents!!. Violet caught one at West Province (Area One), then immediately traded it to Arven for his Mabosstiff.

Trivia

  • Scovillain is the only Fire-type Pokémon in the Grass Egg Group, and the only green Fire-type Pokémon.
  • Scovillain is the only Pokémon with Chlorophyll that is not weak to Fire-type attacks, which have increased power under harsh sunlight.
  • Excluding Legendary Pokémon, whose names are often the same in all localizations of Pokémon games, Scovillain is the only Generation IX Pokémon whose English and Japanese names are the same.
  • Of all Pokémon (or Pokémon evolutionary lines) who formerly had unique type combinations, Scovillain held its unique typing for the shortest period of time. When Pokémon Scarlet and Violet launched on November 18, 2022, Scovillain's Grass/Fire type was unique, but it lost this distinction with the release of The Teal Mask and Hearthflame Mask Ogerpon on September 13, 2023 — 299 days later.
  • Mega Scovillain is the only Pokémon with a base stat total of 586.

Origin

Scovillain seems to be based on a chili pepper plant. The red head seems to be based on a ripe, mature chili, while the green head seems to represent an immature chili that is not as pungent. The shape of the heads seems to be based on the habanero (as alluded to by its Japanese category) or the Carolina Reaper (the spiciest chili in the world at the time of debut).

Scovillain's red head being the only one to produce spicy and fire energy may allude to the Padrón pepper, a variety of pepper cultivated in Galicia, Spain. The flavor of these peppers can vary from mild to very spicy, which has given way to the Galician saying "Os pementos de Padrón, uns pican e outros non" ("Padrón peppers, some are hot, some are not"). Scovillain's appearance and behavior also suggest inspiration from villains in Western films; in particular, its typical stance recalls the classic hands-on-belt pose common among cowboys and the gunslinger's pre-duel stance before a showdown. All of this, together with its Paldean origin, may reference Spain's role as a major filming location for many iconic Spaghetti Westerns.

Its polarized expressions may be based on the sock and buskin theater masks, with the green and red heads representing Thalia (muse of comedy) and Melpomene (muse of tragedy), respectively.

Mega Scovillain seems to be wearing a suit jacket and a necktie. The "necktie" also resembles a stylized chili pepper. Its spikes help give it the appearance of a dinosaur or a kaiju.

Name origin

Scovillain may be a combination of Scoville (as in the Scoville scale, the measurement scale for chili pepper pungency) and villain.
